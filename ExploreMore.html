Fun Facts about Carbon emissions (part1):

•Meat has a bigger carbon footprint than other types of foods like grains or vegetables. That’s because animals like cattle and sheep produce lots of methane gas. In 2016 they produced 170 million tons of methane gas according to the Centre for Sustainable Systems at the University of Michigan.

•Other dairy products make uparound 19 % - worse than poultry,seafood and eggs which make up around 14%. Vegetables on the other hand make up a mere 4.9 %.

Fun Facts about Carbon emissions (part2):

• One American household emits 8.1 metric tones of C02e through food consumption alone. Production of the food accounts for 83 % and its transportation accounts for around 11 %.

Fun Facts about Carbon emissions (part3):

•Even just following the speed limit while driving could reduce your carbon footprint, save you a speeding ticket , improve your fuel economy and save you your money. Driving 50 mph or more, every 5 mph increase means paying 20 – 40 cents more per gallon.

•Transportation is the biggest producer of carbon emissions after electricity generation.

Fun Facts about Carbon emissions (part4):

•For every 10 % waste reduction , you can reduce 1,200 pounds of c02e. This doesn’t just include recycling; it includes things like refusing plastic bags at the supermarket.

•Corporations have the biggest carbon footprint. Just 100 of them are responsible for more than 70 % of the world’s emissions according to a 2017 report by from the non-profit CDP in collaboration with Climate Accountability Institute.
